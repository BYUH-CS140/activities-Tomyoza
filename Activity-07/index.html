Tonkatsu

What is Tonkatsu?

Tonkatsu is a Japanese dish made of breaded, deep-fried pork cutlet.
It’s crispy on the outside, tender inside, and usually served with shredded cabbage and a sweet-savory sauce.
It’s a popular comfort food in Japan.

Watch How to Make Tonkatsu

Steps to Make Tonkatsu

  1. Trim the pork and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Coat the pork in flour.
  3. Dip it in beaten egg.
  4. Cover evenly with panko breadcrumbs.
  5. Fry in oil at 170–180°C until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Drain excess oil and serve with shredded cabbage and tonkatsu sauce if desired.

Ingredients

  • 2 pork loin cutlets
  • Salt and pepper (a pinch)
  • Flour (as needed)
  • 1 egg
  • Panko breadcrumbs (as needed)
  • Oil for frying
